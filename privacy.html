Privacy & Data Management Policy

Last updated: 13 Jul 2026 — Effective: 01 Jul 2026

1. Introduction

ikorex provides intelligent process automation services to businesses, including robotic process automation (RPA), WhatsApp-based conversational automation, Order-to-Cash and Procure-to-Pay automation, AI computer vision monitoring, and finance and accounting automation ("Services").

We are committed to protecting personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how we use and disclose it, and how you can access, correct, or raise concerns about it.

This policy applies to:

  • visitors to our website;
  • individuals who submit an enquiry or request a scope valuation;
  • contacts at our client and prospective client organisations; and
  • job applicants.

It does not replace the separate data processing terms we enter into with clients that govern personal information we process on a client's behalf through their deployed automation systems (see Section 8).

2. Personal information we collect

Depending on how you interact with us, we may collect:

  • Contact and identity information: Name, Job title, Company name, Email address, Phone number.
  • Enquiry details: the information you provide in our contact/scope valuation form, including your area of interest and a description of your business's operational context.
  • Website usage information: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, and referral source, collected via Google Analytics and cookies (see Section 5).
  • Communications: records of emails, calls, and messages (including WhatsApp messages, where you contact us via WhatsApp) between you and ikorex.
  • Job applicant information: if you apply for a role with us, your resume, cover letter, and any information you provide during recruitment.

We do not knowingly collect sensitive information (such as health, biometric, or financial account information) through our website. If sensitive information is ever required for a Service engagement, we will seek your separate, explicit consent.

3. How we collect personal information

We collect personal information:

  • directly from you, when you complete a form on our website, email us, call us, or message us;
  • automatically, through cookies and analytics tools when you browse our website; and
  • from publicly available sources (such as a company website or LinkedIn) when researching a prospective client engagement.

Where practicable, we collect personal information directly from the individual concerned. Where we receive personal information from a third party (for example, a colleague submitting an enquiry on behalf of others at their company), we expect that party to have the appropriate authority to share it with us.

4. Why we collect, hold, use and disclose personal information

We collect and use personal information to:

  • respond to enquiries and provide scope valuations or quotes;
  • deliver, support, and improve our Services;
  • communicate with clients and prospective clients, including via WhatsApp where you initiate or consent to that channel;
  • manage recruitment and employment processes;
  • meet legal, regulatory, accounting, and taxation obligations; and
  • for direct marketing about our Services, where you have not opted out (see Section 9).

We do not sell personal information, and we do not disclose it to third parties for their own marketing purposes.

5. Cookies and website analytics

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to operate correctly and to understand how visitors use the site. This may include:

  • Essential cookies: required for the website to function.
  • Analytics cookies: to understand visitor behavior in aggregate.

You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect some website functionality. We display a cookie consent banner on first visit, giving you the option to accept or decline non-essential cookies.

6. WhatsApp and third-party platforms

A core part of our Services involves conversational automation delivered through the WhatsApp Business Platform, operated by Meta Platforms, Inc. ("Meta"). If you or your organisations communicate with us or with an ikorex-deployed automation system via WhatsApp:

  • your messages are transmitted and processed through Meta's infrastructure, in accordance with WhatsApp's Business Terms and Meta's Privacy Policy;
  • Meta may process this data outside Australia, including in the United States; and
  • we retain records of these communications as reasonably necessary to deliver and support the relevant Service.

We recommend reviewing WhatsApp's own privacy terms in addition to this policy before using that channel to communicate with us.

7. Disclosure of personal information, including overseas

We may disclose personal information to:

  • Our hosting provider: ikorex's production automation platform and client data are hosted with an Australian-based provider, with data stored in Australia.
  • Meta Platforms, Inc.: in connection with WhatsApp-based communications (see Section 6). This is currently the primary overseas transfer connected to our Services.
  • Professional advisers: such as our accountants or lawyers, where necessary.
  • Regulators or law enforcement: where required or authorised by law.
  • A prospective buyer or their advisers: in the event of a sale, merger, or restructure of our business, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections.

Overseas disclosure (APP 8): Personal information processed through our production automation platform (including client and customer data handled via RPA, O2C/P2P, and finance automation Services) is hosted in Australia. The only currently disclosed overseas transfer connected to our Services is to Meta Platforms, Inc., in the United States, in connection with WhatsApp-based communications (see Section 6) — this is an inherent part of using the WhatsApp Business Platform and cannot be avoided while that channel is offered. Where we do disclose personal information overseas, we take reasonable steps to ensure the overseas recipient handles it consistently with the Australian Privacy Principles, including through contractual protections where appropriate.

8. Personal information we process on behalf of clients

Where ikorex deploys RPA, WhatsApp automation, or AI computer vision systems (such as PPE detection, retail intelligence, or hospitality analytics) for a client, we may process personal information belonging to our client's customers, suppliers, employees, or site visitors (for example, transaction records, supplier communications, or footage captured by a client's cameras) strictly as a service provider acting on that client's instructions.

In these engagements:

  • our client remains responsible, as the collector, for notifying individuals and obtaining any consents required under the Privacy Act;
  • our computer vision products are designed to e.g. "process footage using anonymised behavioural coordinates rather than identifying individuals" / "avoid facial recognition";
  • production systems that process this information are hosted in Australia (see Section 7), and are kept separate from our development and staging environments; our developers do not use real client or customer data for building or testing. Development and testing use synthetic or de-identified data only;
  • access to production systems is restricted to authorised personnel on a least-privilege basis, with multi-factor authentication and logged access (see Section 10);
  • personal information processed on a client's behalf is handled under the terms of our services agreement with that client, which includes confidentiality and data security obligations; and
  • this website privacy policy does not itself govern that processing; the client's own privacy policy and our services agreement with them do.

9. Direct marketing

We may send you information about our Services if you have enquired with us or otherwise provided your contact details for that purpose. You can opt out at any time by:

  • clicking "unsubscribe" in any marketing email, or
  • contacting us using the details in Section 13.

We will not send direct marketing via WhatsApp unless you have consented to that channel for that purpose.

10. Data security

We take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure, including:

  • hosting production systems in Australia, separate from development and staging environments;
  • individual, multi-factor-authenticated accounts for all staff and developers, no shared logins;
  • restricted, least-privilege access to production systems, with production not directly reachable from the internet;
  • encryption of personal information in transit and at rest;
  • centralised logging of access to production systems and personal information;
  • secrets and credentials (such as API keys and access tokens) held in a dedicated secrets manager, not shared informally; and
  • staff confidentiality obligations and regular review of third-party service providers' security practices.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. If we become aware of a data breach that is likely to result in serious harm, we will comply with our obligations under the Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme, including notifying affected individuals and the OAIC where required.

11. Data retention

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law (for example, tax and corporate record-keeping obligations). When no longer required, we take reasonable steps to destroy or de-identify personal information.

Complaints. If you believe we have breached the Privacy Act or the Australian Privacy Principles, please contact us first using the details below, and we will investigate and respond. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC):
